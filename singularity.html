Overview


I am motivated by the idea that physical objects and intuitive affordance. A box is always associated with opening a hidden object beneath its lead, similarly the tendency of origami paper is to unfold, open up a large space or data points from intricate patterns.
The traditional mode of interaction like a pointer mouse wouldn't suffice. As we need 3D interfaces and tangible interaction systems to meet the demands of emerging media. These systems may or may not follow the UI/UX heuristics.

Can we explore the spatial and intuitive mapping of these interactions ?

Inspiration

Affordance & Visual memory

Affordance

Don Norman talks elaborately objects and their affordance yet to be discovered -
“The word "affordance" was originally invented by the perceptual psychologist J. J. Gibson (1977, 1979) to refer to the actionable properties between the world and an actor (a person or animal). To Gibson, affordances are a relationship. They are a part of nature: they do not have to be visible, known, or desirable. Some affordances are yet to be discovered. Some are dangerous. I suspect that none of us know all the affordances of even everyday objects”

An affordance is the possibility of an action on an object or environment

Visual memory

The second inspiration comes from the affordance of 2D images and 3D objects. I wanted to know about objects in its simplest form, the amazing ability of our brain to remember patterns and shapes. For instance we recognize a meme face and associate ourselves with it unlike renaissance portraits. Can we tap into the affordance of forms in its fundamental level?

Can an object in its minimal and abstract form be recognized faster than complex objects ?
Similarly, the continuous exposure of logos make them more familiar and recognizable.The most famous brands like Coca-cola, Nike, Apple exploit these principles. Though there can be several other factors which influences the brand recognition, we would stick to the basic Gestalt principle.
Can a continuous use of a 3D form make someone get used to a certain interaction ?

Form based interaction

Can we explore the basic geometric forms which are deep-seated in our memory and design intuitive interactions?

Concept

Artificial creativity

"Can we create an intelligent system, for now what I call is an ARTIFICIAL CREATIVE?"

We, as designers follow some specific thought process to reach to a certain decision(But, of course there are exception who don't play by the rule!). Let me put it more clearly, let's say my typical client gives me design brief where he says "I want a sporty logo, with dynamic fonts and color". At first, it seems like a nightmare to any graphic designer. Humans are so used to their muscle memory and the way they perceive a problem after a certain time they find it hard to create a disruptive form, a divine form that no human can ever think of with the given his IQ. So how could we create disruptive designs?
Here comes the Artificial Creative.We give inputs like masculine form(more edge and diagonal),  sporty (spontaneous line quality) , traditional (a color scheme picked of from traditional set of images). Parameters can vary from 5 to 50.our artificial creative could quickly generate forms either 2D or 3D, which can act as a primary base for disruptive form generation. Further, can augment human creativity. possibilities are endless. This is what I call the culmination of creativity and visual thinking A design singularity

Artificial creative can act as a primary base for disruptive form generation. Further, it can augment human creativity

Unconventional medium of communication and Augmented senses



One fine afternoon, I was writing a song picking up my acoustic guitar. I could hear every single note of my guitar, associate the combination of musical notes to a distant memory. As if those notes could transcend me into a space I had never seen, a moment I had never lived, an emotion I had never known earlier. There was something personal and universal about those notes and my guitar in that transient moment.

A video posted by Shib Shankar Sahoo (@shibhash) on



So, I had this idea
What led to communication ? Is it the structure of our brain ? the way we are evolved or our social influence? A brain that could differentiate between “Au” and “ka” (Devanagari script), A brain that could retain all the semantic information, associate them with a memory, form a communication, a brain that would evolve from vedas to artificial intelligence. DO we find a similarity? Humans recognize patterns well the same pattern that stores information in different parts of brain to create a perception of a whole flower. It’s fascinating to think how smelling a flower or listening a music leads to firing neural patterns in brain. Gradually, we recognize and train ourselves to retain those normal yet bizarre informations.

Despite of all research on natural language processing, I still believe that there can be changed something in very fundamental level, the way we communicate with other humans and machine.
We use verbal language to talk to our neighbor, body language to express love and anger,gestures to talk to our smart phones, write codes to train machines. Moreover, there are brilliant assistive technologies that augment human senses.
It seems like as if we are so accustomed the word “communication” itself that we don’t even realize there was a time when humans could differentiate between sounds, remember its pitch and frequency through SENSES.

What if I could translate my thoughts into a system just like I am having a conversation or a trained human who doesn’t use conventional senses of information (the gain and retrieval loop) instead he uses trained augmented senses, create story out of it? Are we just limited by our senses or the capability of human brains which has a limit to perceive things up to a certain point. What if there are other parts of brain which can actually trigger unconventional medium of communication? what if some species who is part of another parabolic disk use that medium sitting right next you?



Light has only the photographic(texture,color) information, one can not differentiate between a spatial 3D wall and a photograph (illusion). Similarly Sound has only spatial(volume) data. Can these be explored as augmented senses

Can we augment our senses, push our brain to a limit where it can recognize and differentiate those sounds (patterns in haptic, light, peripheral vision) ?

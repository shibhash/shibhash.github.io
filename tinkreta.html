Tinkering and Learning Kit Design

Toy design for children | STEM Learning | Problem solving | Spatial awareness | Interaction | Augmented Reality

Overview


Tinkreta is a tinkering tool that can be used to create any geometric form in 2D, 3D , Physical and Virtual space.

In this project, I have tried to explore a modular design principle. The learning kit project blends multiple aspects of visual design, mechanism and interaction. Our automata toy construction kit based on the theme of simulating the movements of endangered animals and thus creating awareness and empathy in children towards the natural world.
Team members : Shib S. Sahoo & Ekta S. Surender

"Tinkreta" is derived from the words "Tinker" and "Automata"

Tinkreta

Existing science educational kits



-Based on constructionism
-Simulating the machines around us
-Consist of a number of loose parts which can be joined together to construct a car, truck or crane etc.
-Other tinkering kits e.g. integrated electronics toys for creating programmable robot


Pros and Cons of existing science educational kits

-Parts can be joined together and can not be integrated with the other objects around a child such as a cardboard, sticks
-Missing surrounding interaction or Jugad ability
-Lack of flexibility parts, and its tinkering ability in kits Induce a fear of unknown e.g. intimidating parts leads to lack of curiosity to build things
-Do not nurture interest of a child towards nature


Why they are important?

-Making use of the environment/ surrounding
-Invention, exploration, creativity
-Improve STEM learning in children
-Develop spatial awareness
-Mechanical reasoning
-Critical Thinking
